the year 1999 is the official start-up date of the euro, the common european currency that will unite 11 countries monetarily. the 11 countries launching the euro are austria, belgium, ireland, italy, finland, france, germany, luxembourg, the netherlands, spain and portugal.
 denmark, sweden and britain decided to stay out of the euro for political reasons. according to the european union, britain has twice as many eu citizens as france, but it is not the country with the highest concentration of residents from other european countries.
